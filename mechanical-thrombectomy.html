Mechanical Thrombectomy

A stepwise, algorithm-led guide to selecting patients with large-vessel occlusion for endovascular thrombectomy (EVT), with the trial evidence behind each decision.

Large-vessel occlusion Endovascular therapy Time & imaging selection

Educational resource for healthcare professionals. A learning aid — not a substitute for the acute stroke rota, neurointervention/neuroradiology assessment, or local protocols. Eligibility criteria are specific; always confirm against the source trials and your local pathway. This page complements the Ischaemic Stroke page and the Stroke Scores (ASPECTS and mRS) page.

1. The core question

Thrombectomy is considered for large-vessel occlusion (LVO) causing a disabling deficit. Selection weighs, in combination:

  • Occlusion site — is there a treatable proximal occlusion (ICA, M1, selected M2; basilar)?
  • Time from last-known-well — early (0–6 h) vs extended (6–24 h) window.
  • Core vs penumbra — how much brain is already infarcted (ASPECTS / core volume) versus salvageable (perfusion mismatch)?
  • Baseline function and age — pre-morbid mRS, age, and overall candidacy.

Each factor is expanded below, then combined into the master algorithm.

2. Occlusion site

  • Internal carotid artery (ICA) and proximal middle cerebral artery (M1): the best-established indication.
  • M2 (and selected M3): thrombectomy is reasonable for proximal, disabling M2 occlusions; benefit is less certain the more distal you go.
  • Basilar artery: treat actively — poor natural history; supported by ATTENTION and BAOCHE (see below).
  • Distal / medium-vessel occlusions (MeVO): routine thrombectomy not established (recent MeVO trials neutral). Individualise.

Vessel imaging (CT angiography from arch to vertex) is essential and must include the posterior circulation.

3. Time windows

  • 0–6 hours (anterior LVO): thrombectomy is standard of care on the basis of the 2015 trials pooled in HERMES — advanced perfusion imaging is not mandatory to treat in this window if ASPECTS is adequate.
  • 6–24 hours (anterior LVO): selection becomes imaging-based, requiring perfusion (CTP) or DWI/perfusion MRI to demonstrate salvageable tissue — per DAWN and DEFUSE-3.
  • Basilar occlusion: consider up to 24 hours (ATTENTION within 12 h; BAOCHE 6–24 h).

4. Core, penumbra and the mismatch concept

Ischaemic core = already-infarcted, non-salvageable tissue. Penumbra = hypoperfused but still-salvageable tissue. The bigger the penumbra relative to the core (the mismatch), the more there is to save.

DEFUSE-3 perfusion criteria (6–16 h)

  • Ischaemic core volume less than 70 mL
  • Mismatch ratio ≥ 1.8 (penumbra-to-core)
  • Absolute mismatch volume ≥ 15 mL

DAWN clinical–core mismatch (6–24 h)

Pairs deficit severity (NIHSS) with a small core, e.g.:

  • Age 80 or over: NIHSS ≥ 10 and core < 21 mL
  • Age under 80: NIHSS ≥ 10 and core < 31 mL; or NIHSS ≥ 20 and core 31–51 mL

(Core volume is usually estimated on automated CT-perfusion software.)

5. ASPECTS — the imaging estimate of core

ASPECTS (Alberta Stroke Program Early CT Score) is a 10-point score of early ischaemic change across the MCA territory — 10 = normal, lower = more established infarct (see the dedicated ASPECTS page for the region map and worked scoring).

  • ASPECTS 6–10: generally favourable for thrombectomy.
  • ASPECTS 3–5 (large core): thrombectomy is still beneficial in this range on the basis of the 2022–2024 large-core trials (RESCUE-Japan LIMIT, SELECT2, ANGEL-ASPECT, TENSION, plus TESLA, LASTE).
  • ASPECTS 0–2 (very extensive core): benefit uncertain/limited — individualise.

6. Baseline function (mRS) and age

Trials generally enrolled patients with good pre-morbid function (mRS 0–1, some to mRS 2). A poor baseline (e.g. mRS 3–5) makes benefit less likely and shifts toward individualised decisions. See the modified Rankin Scale.

Age is not, by itself, a barrier — older patients benefited in the extended-window and large-core trials — but combine age with baseline function, core size and goals of care.

9. Key teaching points

In summary

  • LVO + disabling deficit + good baseline (mRS 0–2) = assess for thrombectomy urgently.
  • 0–6 h: treat on ASPECTS; 6–24 h: need perfusion/DWI mismatch (DEFUSE-3 or DAWN numbers).
  • Large core (ASPECTS 3–5) is no longer an automatic exclusion.
  • Basilar occlusion: act, even in extended windows.
  • Give thrombolysis if eligible — thrombectomy does not replace it.
