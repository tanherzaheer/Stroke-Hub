Stroke Scores: ASPECTS and the Modified Rankin Scale (mRS)

Two scores used constantly in acute stroke decisions — one for the size of the infarct core on CT, one for global disability and dependency.

ASPECTS 0–10 mRS 0–6 Selection & outcome

Educational resource for healthcare professionals. The diagrams below are original schematic illustrations for teaching — they are simplified and not a substitute for a radiologist's read of the actual scan. A learning aid only; verify against current guidance.

1. ASPECTS (Alberta Stroke Program Early CT Score)

1.1 What it is

ASPECTS is a 10-point topographic score that quantifies early ischaemic change in the middle cerebral artery (MCA) territory on a non-contrast CT head. You start at 10 (normal) and subtract 1 point for each of the 10 defined regions showing early ischaemic change (loss of grey–white differentiation, hypoattenuation, sulcal effacement).

  • ASPECTS 10 = no visible early ischaemic change.
  • Lower score = more established infarct = larger core.

ASPECTS is assessed on two axial levels: the ganglionic level (basal ganglia/thalamus) and the supraganglionic level (just above).

1.2 The 10 regions

  • Ganglionic level: C (caudate), L (lentiform nucleus), IC (internal capsule), I (insular ribbon), M1, M2, M3 (anterior, lateral, posterior MCA cortex at this level).
  • Supraganglionic level: M4, M5, M6 (anterior, lateral, posterior MCA cortex just above the ganglionic level).

1.3 Schematic region map

1.4 How it's used

ASPECTS helps estimate core size on plain CT when perfusion imaging isn't available.

  • 6–10: generally favourable for reperfusion therapy.
  • 3–5 (large core): thrombectomy is still beneficial (2022–2024 large-core trials).
  • 0–2: very large core; benefit uncertain.

A posterior-circulation equivalent (pc-ASPECTS) exists for the vertebrobasilar territory. See the Mechanical Thrombectomy page for how ASPECTS combines with time window, occlusion site and mismatch.

2. Modified Rankin Scale (mRS)

2.1 What it is

The mRS is the standard global disability / dependency scale after stroke, running from 0 (no symptoms) to 6 (death). It is the primary outcome in most stroke trials and is used both to describe pre-morbid function (which informs treatment decisions) and outcome.

The modified Rankin Scale, 0–6
ScoreMeaning
0No symptoms at all
1No significant disability — able to carry out all usual activities despite some symptoms
2Slight disability — unable to carry out all previous activities but able to look after own affairs without assistance
3Moderate disability — requires some help, but able to walk unassisted
4Moderately severe disability — unable to walk or attend to bodily needs without assistance
5Severe disability — bedridden, incontinent, requiring constant nursing care and attention
6Dead

2.2 Visual scale

2.3 How it's used

  • Pre-morbid mRS 0–2 is the typical threshold for offering thrombectomy (trials mostly enrolled mRS 0–1).
  • Outcome is often dichotomised as "functional independence" = mRS 0–2, or "reduced disability" as a shift across the whole scale.
