Stroke Algorithms and Decision Maps

Visual, evidence-based decision maps — young/cryptogenic workup, the sickle cell pathway, thrombectomy eligibility, hemicraniectomy, and ICH surgery — rendered as flowcharts you can follow branch by branch.

Decision support Mermaid flowcharts Teaching tools

How to use these maps

These flowcharts summarise a reasonable evidence-based approach but simplify complex decisions. They do not replace senior/specialist review, local protocols, or clinical judgement. Every branch assumes appropriate imaging, exclusion of contraindications, and MDT input where relevant.

1. Young / cryptogenic TIA or ischaemic stroke — investigation pathway

Covers a patient under roughly 55 with TIA or ischaemic stroke, from the immediate antiplatelet decision through cause-finding — including the role and timing of catheter angiography and posterior-circulation nuances.

Notes on catheter angiography (DSA)

Non-invasive imaging (CTA/MRA) is first line. DSA is reserved for when non-invasive imaging is inconclusive but the clinical picture suggests primary CNS vasculitis, subtle arterial dissection, vascular malformation (AVM/fistula), or aneurysm.

Posterior circulation — do not under-investigate. Vertebral artery dissection is a leading cause of posterior-circulation stroke in younger patients, and vertebrobasilar anatomy can be harder to assess non-invasively — a low threshold for dedicated vessel imaging (and DSA if unresolved) is appropriate.

2. Sickle cell disease and stroke — acute and preventive pathway

Key trials: STOP (chronic transfusion cuts first-stroke risk in children with abnormal TCD by ~90%), STOP2 (do not discontinue transfusion — risk returns), TWiTCH (hydroxycarbamide non-inferior to transfusion for lowering TCD velocities in selected children), and the ASH 2020 guidelines (exchange transfusion for acute neurological events).

3. Mechanical thrombectomy — eligibility map

From confirmation of large-vessel occlusion through time windows, imaging mismatch, and large-core selection.

Distal / medium-vessel occlusion: routine thrombectomy is not established (recent MeVO trials such as ESCAPE-MeVO and DISTAL did not show clear benefit) — individualise.

4. Malignant MCA infarction — decompressive hemicraniectomy

Selecting patients with a large MCA-territory infarct for decompressive surgery, and how age shapes the expected benefit.

Cerebellar infarction with brainstem compression or hydrocephalus is a separate emergency — suboccipital decompression ± EVD; escalate to neurosurgery urgently.

5. Intracerebral haemorrhage (ICH) — acute management and surgery

The acute medical bundle, anticoagulant reversal by agent, and location-based surgical decisions.

6. References (key trials referenced in the maps)

  1. Thrombectomy: HERMES (Lancet 2016); DAWN, DEFUSE-3 (NEJM 2018); RESCUE-Japan LIMIT (NEJM 2022); SELECT2, ANGEL-ASPECT (NEJM 2023); TENSION (Lancet 2023); ATTENTION, BAOCHE (NEJM 2022/2023).
  2. Hemicraniectomy: DECIMAL/DESTINY/HAMLET pooled (Lancet Neurology 2007); DESTINY II (NEJM 2014).
  3. ICH: INTERACT2 (NEJM 2013); ATACH-2 (NEJM 2016); PATCH (Lancet 2016); STICH (Lancet 2005); STICH II (Lancet 2013); MISTIE III (Lancet 2019); ENRICH (NEJM 2024); SWITCH (Lancet 2024).
  4. Sickle cell: STOP (NEJM 1998); STOP2 (NEJM 2005); TWiTCH (Lancet 2016); ASH 2020 cerebrovascular guidelines (Blood Advances 2020).

These maps are teaching tools. Confirm every decision against current national/local guidelines and senior clinical input.
