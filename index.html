Who is this for?

Stroke Hub is written for healthcare professionals — medical students, foundation and resident doctors, stroke physicians, nurses, and allied health staff — who want a quick, well-organised reference. It is not intended for members of the public seeking personal medical advice; if you or someone else may be having a stroke, call your local emergency number immediately.

About this site

Stroke Hub is an open, static educational website built with plain HTML, CSS, and a small amount of JavaScript. Content is organised so that new topic pages can be added easily — each page follows a consistent template with a table of contents, structured sections, and a references list, making the site easy to extend and easy for search engines to index.

Content is drawn from major clinical guidelines (e.g. NICE, AHA/ASA, European Stroke Organisation) and landmark trials, and is intended as a study aid and quick-reference summary — always confirm current practice against your local guidelines and the latest primary literature.

Disclaimer: Stroke Hub is provided for educational purposes only and is intended for healthcare professionals. It is not a substitute for clinical judgement, local protocols, or up-to-date primary literature, and must not be used as the sole basis for patient care decisions. In a medical emergency, contact your local emergency services immediately.
