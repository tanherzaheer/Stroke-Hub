Stroke Hub

An educational reference on stroke medicine for healthcare professionals — covering acute assessment, ischaemic and haemorrhagic stroke, secondary prevention, and decision algorithms, drawn from UK, US and European guidelines and the major clinical trials.

Educational resource only. Not a substitute for full guidelines, local trust protocols, the BNF, or clinical judgement. Always verify against current national and local guidance before making clinical decisions.

Who is this for?

Stroke Hub is written for healthcare professionals — medical students, foundation and resident doctors, stroke physicians, nurses, and allied health staff — who want a quick, well-organised reference. It is not intended for members of the public seeking personal medical advice; if you or someone else may be having a stroke, call your local emergency number immediately.

Disclaimer: Stroke Hub is an educational resource for healthcare professionals and is not a substitute for professional guidelines, local protocols, clinical judgement, or up-to-date primary literature, and must not be used as the sole basis for patient care decisions. Content is maintained by its author and may not reflect the most recent guideline updates (last reviewed: July 2026). In a medical emergency, contact your local emergency services immediately.
